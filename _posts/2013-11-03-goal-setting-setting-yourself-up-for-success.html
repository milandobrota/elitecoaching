Make your long term goals big and compelling. You need a strong enough driver to make your efforts worthwhile.

Don't worry about how you are going to do it right away. Allow yourself to identify what you truly want first.

Once you realize what that is, brainstorm. Come up with a plan to make it happen.

Once you have the plan in place, set your daily goals. Contrary to your long term goals, daily goals should be very simple and easy to accomplish. Expecting too much causes resentment.

Tim Ferriss sets out to write two crappy pages a day. Overnight success is an illusion. It's always one small, simple step at a time.

Completing these simple daily goals will increase your confidence and momentum. Often times, you will find yourself doing more than expected, which will boost your morale and eventually create a more sustainable system.

Small steps lead to big success.
