5 Ways To Improve Your Productivity


Photo by: Sigurd Decroos

Facebook's COO, Sheryl Sandberg, leaves work at 5:30PM. Working longer hours does not always mean you're going to get more stuff done.

Don't confuse effort with results. Also, don't confuse time with productivity. Productive people get things done in less time, not more.

Here's 5 ways to maximize your productivity:

1. Learn to say no

The fastest way to be done with the task is to not do it at all. Learn to say no to things that don't matter. Reevaluate your commitments regularly.

2. Do the right thing

Know the difference between urgent and important. Make a decision. Not everything can be high priority.

3. Timeboxing

Once you decide what you want to do, use the Pomodoro technique to limit how much time you have available. This will force you to focus, and make it easier for you to get in the flow.

4. Discipline

Let's say you want to write a book. An average book has 64,000 words. If you were to write 500 words (around 2 pages - not that much!) every day, you would be done in 4 months! In a year, you would write 3 books!!!

5. Health

Being healthy will make you feel better, look better and be more confident.

Eating too much will make you tired. Have many smaller meals throughout the day, and avoid sugar.

Sleeping too little will hurt your creativity. Know how much sleep you need, and make sure you get that much sleep every night. If you need to wake up early, go to bed early. As simple as that.

Exercise will boost your energy and improve your mood. Do it a couple of days a week, even if it's only for 15 minutes and very soon you will notice a difference in how you feel.

Put Off Procrastination - Tip #3: Avoid Perfectionism


Photo by: Sergey Nivens

Stop being so hard on yourself! Demanding so much from yourself will make you want to stop before you even start.

And even if you manage to trick yourself into taking action, you will have trouble being happy with what you've created because you can always find something "wrong".

Stop sabotaging yourself.

I'm not saying you should stop striving for quality in the products or relationships you create or the way you spend your time. That's not the point.

What I'm saying is: Don't ignore the positive things. If you constantly focus on what's missing, you will never be happy. Your brain will link your goals to pain and before you know it, you will give up.

Instead, learn to be happy even if things aren't perfect. Learn to value yourself more. Be proud of your hard work and pat yourself on the back.

If the results are not there yet, don't worry. Over time, you will get better.

Don't Fall For These 3 Planning Mistakes

Mistake #1: Never being ready

Planning is great. But how much is too much? How can you stop over-planning?

To answer these questions, you need to know what problem you are trying to solve. Why do we plan in the first place?

Planning provides us with the tactics and confidence. If you are not sure what you should do to make progress, you need to come up with the tactics. If you don't think you're good enough to do something, despite spending a lot of time preparing and working on your skills, you need to work on your confidence.

Preparing can go a long way toward helping you become more confident, but achieving high levels of confidence will require that you perform the task. To be good at something, you need to do it. Not just read about it, or plan indefinitely. Start right now, or figure out exactly what you need to do before you do.

Mistake #2: Planning too far ahead

How much can you really predict? Not that much, really. So plan accordingly. You may feel confident about the things in the near future. Focus on your next move. You can try anticipating what might happen in the near future, but don't expect your plan to handle every possible variation years from now.

The plan is not the end goal. There is no guarantee of success. Don't be shortsighted. If you execute the plan perfectly, you did a great job following the plan, but don't be surprised if the results don't reflect your efforts.

Mistake #3: Making no adjustments

Reality changes. The only constant thing in the world is change. What might be a good idea now, might not be a good idea later on.

And not just that. Reality is different from how we experience it. We all have our own "reality" and in our heads, a plan might work out perfectly, but the truth is, you won't know until you go ahead and do it.

Iterate! Make a reasonable plan and execute it. Then reflect on what worked and what didn't and adjust the sails accordingly. The success might come sooner than you think.
